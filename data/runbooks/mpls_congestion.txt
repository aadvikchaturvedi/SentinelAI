RUNBOOK: MPLS Congestion
ID: RB-001
Severity: High

DESCRIPTION:
MPLS congestion occurs when traffic demand exceeds the available bandwidth on MPLS links,
causing latency spikes, packet loss, and jitter affecting all applications running over the link.

SYMPTOMS:
- Latency above 80ms on primary MPLS links
- Bandwidth utilization above 85%
- Packet loss above 2%
- Jitter above 20ms
- User complaints about slow ERP and video call quality

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Identify the congested MPLS segment from telemetry dashboard.
2. Shift non-critical traffic (file transfers, email) to alternate tunnel immediately.
3. Prioritize ERP and Video traffic using QoS policies.
4. Enable traffic shaping on the congested interface.
5. Notify affected site managers within 5 minutes.

REROUTING STEPS:
1. Check Tunnel-B availability and current utilization.
2. If Tunnel-B is below 70% utilization, reroute primary ERP traffic to Tunnel-B.
3. Monitor latency every 2 minutes after rerouting.
4. If latency drops below 30ms within 10 minutes, rerouting was successful.

ESCALATION:
- If congestion persists beyond 20 minutes: escalate to Network Lead.
- If ERP becomes unreachable: escalate to NOC Manager immediately.
- If multiple sites affected: initiate Major Incident process.

RESOLUTION VERIFICATION:
- Latency below 30ms for 5 consecutive minutes.
- Packet loss below 0.5%.
- All application teams confirm normal operations.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
Incident #42 (2024-03): Hub congestion resolved in 12 minutes by rerouting to Tunnel-B.
Latency reduced by 40% within 8 minutes of rerouting.
