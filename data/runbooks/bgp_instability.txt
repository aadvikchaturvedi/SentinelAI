RUNBOOK: BGP Route Instability
ID: RB-003
Severity: High

DESCRIPTION:
BGP route flapping causes repeated withdrawal and re-advertisement of routes, leading to
intermittent connectivity and increased CPU load on routing devices.

SYMPTOMS:
- BGP event count above 10 in 5 minutes
- Routes being withdrawn and re-advertised repeatedly
- Increased CPU on hub and datacenter routers
- Intermittent connectivity to specific sites

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Check BGP neighbor state on all hub and datacenter routers.
2. Identify which BGP sessions are flapping.
3. Apply route dampening to suppress flapping routes temporarily.
4. Check for interface errors or physical link issues on BGP peering links.

STABILIZATION STEPS:
1. Apply BGP route dampening with half-life of 5 minutes.
2. Monitor BGP event count — should reduce to below 2 per minute within 10 minutes.
3. Do NOT reset BGP sessions unless absolutely necessary (causes brief outage).

ESCALATION:
- If flapping involves upstream ISP BGP session: contact ISP NOC immediately.
- If route table size increases abnormally: check for BGP route injection attack.

RESOLUTION VERIFICATION:
- BGP event count below 1 per 5 minutes for 10 consecutive minutes.
- All expected routes present in routing table.
- No route withdrawals in last 5 minutes.
